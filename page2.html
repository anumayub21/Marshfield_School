Chapel Green Board School

About

Links for sources available for this school are at the end of the text.

Chapel Green district was populated largely by people in the textile or mining occupations. It could be described as a poor area by today’s standards. Education in the immediate area consisted of the Chapel Green Church School, which appears to have been overcrowded and understaffed. Other schools were about a mile away.

Residents in the area petitioned the School Board to open another school in the area. A survey of school accommodation in the area identified that there was a deficiency of 370 places.

Subsequently a new school was opened on Mon 10 Sept 1877 in rented premises within the same building as the Church School, which is now a Hindu Temple

The new school was called Chapel Green Board School and had an Infants and Junior (Mixed) departments. They occupied the ground floor (see the plan). whilst the Church School had the upper floor (see the plan).

The school was to become popular and achieved good attainments. After being open a few year the school began to run out of space and soon various lack of space issues were being reported in the government annual inspection reports. The School Board started discussions about building a new school rather than renting larger promises. Church members on the School Board were opposed to building a new school. Eventually building a new school was voted on and the search for a site, design and quotes began. This school closed on 29/10/1886

Notable Dates

1877 – Chapel Green School opened on 10 Sept 1877 .

1880 – Elementary education was made compulsory for 5 – 13-year-olds .

1880 – Minimum leaving age was a 10, providing they had attained standard 5.

1880 – Part time schooling allowed if they had attained standard 2
